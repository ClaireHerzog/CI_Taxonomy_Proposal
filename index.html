Purpose & context Claire works at Corporate Insight, a financial services research firm, and is leading a taxonomy modernization initiative. The core goal is replacing legacy SharePoint term stores with a new ANSI/NISO Z39.19-compliant taxonomy organized around the customer journey. Success looks like stakeholder alignment, a formal roadmap, resource commitment, and sustainable governance (including a designated Taxonomy Manager role). The project sits at the intersection of information architecture, content strategy, and SharePoint/document management systems. Claire has strong working knowledge of taxonomy standards (ANSI/NISO Z39.19, ISO 25964-1), controlled vocabulary principles (BT/NT/RT/UF relationships), and financial services terminology. Corporate Insight's research products are referred to as "monitors." Current state The taxonomy has been built out as an 8-category structure aligned to the customer journey: Customer Acquisition, Customer Onboarding, Account Information, Planning and Education, Account Servicing, Transactions and Money Movement, Customer Support, and Digital Experience. Legacy term mapping has been completed with varying confidence levels, with a significant portion of terms (primarily entity/reference data like Firms and Educational Institutions) flagged for manual resolution and recommended as separate controlled facets rather than forcing them into the new taxonomy structure. Claire has presented the initiative to her CEO, with three primary asks: roadmap approval, Q3 resource commitment, and formal designation of a Taxonomy Manager role. Deliverables prepared for that meeting included a branded slide deck and a two-page leave-behind document. An interactive HTML-based taxonomy browser has been developed as a project artifact, currently featuring: Hierarchical browsing with clickable narrower terms (NT modal popups) Dark/light mode toggle Search functionality CI brand colour palette (Navy #1D2851, Teal #215D64, Sage #56878C) Monitor assignments across all taxonomy sections Document auto-tagging simulation with confidence scoring Recent work removed several features from the browser (monitor filter, tag document function, export function, colorblind checkbox) to simplify the interface. On the horizon Full migration from legacy SharePoint term stores to the new taxonomy Completing ANSI/NISO Z39.19 and ISO 25964-1 compliance gaps Populating remaining term definitions, scope notes, and use cases (Claire prefers to fill these in herself using domain expertise and existing glossary documents) Governance establishment for ongoing taxonomy management Potential parallel running period during SharePoint migration Key learnings & principles Entity/reference data (firms, institutions) should be maintained as separate controlled facets rather than forced into a content taxonomy structure — a key architectural decision reached during the mapping process The taxonomy is structured around the customer journey, which serves as the organizing principle for the 8-category framework Claire prefers a collaborative approach where Claude provides technical frameworks and structural scaffolding, while she populates domain-specific content (definitions, use cases, scope notes) When making code changes to the taxonomy browser, Claire prefers targeted snippets with precise placement instructions over complete rewrites Approach & patterns Works iteratively on the taxonomy browser, requesting specific feature additions or removals rather than full system overhauls Uses Excel workbooks with multiple structured tabs (mapping, summary, review needed, reference) as a working format for term management Presentation materials follow CI brand standards (navy/teal palette); Claire has provided the full hex palette for consistent use When preparing leadership communications, Claire needs both a slide deck and a concise leave-behind document Tools & resources SharePoint (legacy term stores being replaced) React-based HTML taxonomy browser (primary interactive artifact) Excel for term mapping and audit tracking PowerPoint and Word for stakeholder communications Standards: ANSI/NISO Z39.19, ISO 25964-1 CI brand colour palette: White #FFFFFF, Navy #1D2851, Teal #215D64, Sage #56878C (plus extended variations) Monitor list (financial services research product lines): Bank, Credit Card, Lending, Small Business, Health System, Health Plan, Medicare, Pharmacy, Asset Management – Investor, Asset Management – Advisor, Retirement – Institutional, Workplace Finance, Mobile, Annuity, Life, Property and Casualty, Broker, Automated Investing, e-Monitor, Fintech

Project Proposal

A Four-Phase Modernization Plan

The migration is structured as a phased rollout that allows parallel system operation, stakeholder validation, and iterative refinement — minimizing disruption to active content workflows while building toward full ISO 25964-1:2011 compliance.

1
Phase 1

Foundation & Audit

Months 1–2
  • Finalize proposed ANSI/NISO Z39.19 taxonomy structure
  • Complete term mapping across all six legacy data sources
  • Resolve 706 terms flagged for manual review
  • Remediate 129 firm tag inconsistencies
  • Deliver governance documentation v1
2
Phase 2

Authoring Site Remediation

Months 2–4
  • Address 128 HIGH-priority authoring issues first
  • Rename collections, libraries, and content types
  • Apply consistent taxonomy across 7 monitor categories
  • Train content authoring team on new terms
  • QA pass with stakeholders
3
Phase 3

Client Site & Navigation

Months 4–6
  • Remediate 30 HIGH-priority client navigation issues
  • Harmonize labels across 26+ client-facing monitors
  • Align portal taxonomy with new controlled vocabulary
  • Run parallel systems during transition
  • Gather client feedback and iterate
4
Phase 4

Full Migration & Cutover

Months 6–9
  • Decommission legacy SharePoint term stores
  • Deploy production taxonomy browser
  • Enable AI-powered document tagging
  • Achieve full ISO 25964-1:2011 compliance
  • Establish ongoing governance cadence

Ongoing Operations

What It Takes to Keep This Running

A taxonomy is a living system, not a one-time deliverable. Sustained quality requires defined roles, recurring review cycles, and proactive monitoring — especially as Corporate Insight's monitor portfolio and covered firm universe continue to grow.

🔄

Continuous Term Management

Every new monitor, product feature, or market entrant potentially requires new taxonomy terms or modification of existing ones. This cannot be ad hoc.

  • New financial services terms as markets evolve
  • New firm tags as coverage expands
  • Deprecation of obsolete terms
  • Broader/narrower term relationship updates
  • Definition and scope note revisions
🏢

Firm Tag Stewardship

The firm facet is dynamic. Financial services firms merge, rebrand, spin off, and launch constantly. Ongoing stewardship prevents the firm tag problem from recurring.

  • Monitor M&A activity and rebrand announcements
  • Add newly covered firms on intake
  • Redirect deprecated tags to canonical forms
  • Maintain abbreviation / alternate name mappings
  • Audit for newly discovered duplicates
📊

Quality Monitoring

Automated and manual quality checks ensure taxonomy integrity is maintained across both the authoring and client-facing environments.

  • Orphaned tag detection and cleanup
  • Untagged or under-tagged document reporting
  • Cross-monitor naming consistency audits
  • Client search & filter feedback review
  • Compliance spot-checks against ISO 25964-1
🎓

Author Training & Support

Content authors are the first line of taxonomy application. Without training and clear guidance, inconsistencies will re-emerge even after the migration is complete.

  • New hire onboarding for the taxonomy system
  • Reference documentation and quick guides
  • Tag suggestion review process for ambiguous cases
  • Feedback channel for authors to flag issues
⚙️

System & Technical Upkeep

The taxonomy browser and document tagging system require ongoing technical maintenance to stay current with platform updates and expanding use cases.

  • SharePoint term store synchronization
  • Taxonomy browser feature updates
  • CSV export and API access maintenance
  • AI tagging model calibration as terms evolve

Governance Framework

Roles, Responsibilities & Decision Rights

Sustainable taxonomy governance requires clear ownership at every level — from day-to-day term stewardship to structural decisions that affect all monitors and the client experience.

Role Responsibility Cadence Decision Authority
Taxonomy Manager Day-to-day term management, author support, quality monitoring, firm tag stewardship Weekly / ongoing Add/modify narrower terms and firm tags; escalate structural changes
Information Architect Taxonomy structure oversight, ISO compliance, system documentation, tool maintenance Monthly Approve new top-level nodes; define hierarchy rules; manage compliance standards
Monitor Leads Identify domain-specific term needs, validate proposed changes within their monitor scope Monthly / on-request Recommend terms; approve changes affecting their monitor's content
Content Authors Apply taxonomy tags during document creation; flag ambiguities and gaps Ongoing Apply existing approved terms; submit requests for new terms
Governance Committee Major structural decisions, priority-setting, cross-monitor impact assessment Quarterly Full authority over taxonomy structure, policy changes, and tool investments
Technology / Dev Maintain SharePoint term stores, taxonomy browser, API integrations, and tagging tools Monthly / as needed Technical implementation decisions; platform migration scope
